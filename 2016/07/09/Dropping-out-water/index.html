Dropping-out water

Jul 09, 2016

Its long after I visited my home in scorching summer this year in country side. Several years back there were greenrees in this season which is not visible today. I witnessed wells, ponds and nearby river running out of water. The practice of houling drinking water is over now and people use handpumps and tubewells for usage of water. This is depend on the availability of electricity. Any way they are used to in storing and managing water. Some times usually in night wild animals hover around the houses in search of water. Still today, sorrounding my home, grooves of tall trees/orchards of mangoes,blue berry, peepal, neem and other shedy trees can be seen under which herds of cattle settle during hot summer days along with herders. They wait for the Sun to alight before they spread around for grazing.

The climatic change if I would be right to mention has an impetus on the habitat of the region as people are rationally involved in their practice of utilizing water as they used to do long back. The abundance of water reserves went scarce only to the climatic change. People are adapted with the changing situations. It seems as if they are not worried. This phenamenon at large scale may lead to the drought and starvation. Which I witnessed at one village can be the scene of large populated areas which in turn may take tough managerial practice by the governments itself.

Since in the cities use of water is minimal and is limited to their day today use for bathing cleaning etc. Factories use and recycle waste water. People hardly find any kitchen garden to irrigate but the eco friendly people decorate their flower pots. However in the villages cultivating field and crops need lot of water. If its done the season, the need of extra is over. However with the spreading awareness agriculturists are more alert. Even then use of no proper water nullas/pipes and percolating water waste at some extent is concern.

Human practice is also is the scene in villages where people install pucca treches filled with water for stray animals and birds. May God live with them ! We have also the sope of spreading awareness with the world pacing fast in technology. And communicate living with them in global village.
